\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented a dual-mode multimodal NBA recommendation system that mines
text, visual, and behavioural multimedia data to unify active search and
passive personalisation within a single architecture.
The work addresses the two-phase project scope: Phase~1 established the
data collection and preprocessing pipeline, extracted multimodal features
via BGE-M3 and CLIP, and evaluated NBA models across multiple data sources;
Phase~2 delivered the full integrated system combining Cleora behavioural
graphs with DIF-SASRec sequential personalisation, along with end-to-end
evaluation and comparison against baseline approaches.

By grounding DIF-SASRec in dense BGE-M3 content embeddings rather than
item IDs, the system generalises to the full 3.08M-item catalog without
retraining.
The Cleora behavioural pipeline provides robust cold-start recommendations
while DIF-SASRec delivers fine-grained sequential personalisation via
online adaptation.
Experiments on 100k Amazon Books users demonstrate HR@10 of 0.7749
(+78\% over the content-only baseline), with warm-path P50 latency of
34\,ms and safe concurrent inference via the AgentPool.

Future work includes dynamic Cleora retraining from accumulated
interaction logs, expansion of multilingual keyword search beyond
the current Tantivy index, cross-domain transfer of pretrained
DIF-SASRec weights, and extension of the NBA framing to domains
beyond book discovery.
